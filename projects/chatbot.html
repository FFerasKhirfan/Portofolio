Business Card Reader using OCR

An intelligent application that scans business cards, extracts contact information using OCR, and organizes data into a digital contacts database.

Python OpenCV Tesseract OCR Flask JavaScript HTML/CSS Computer Vision Regular Expressions

Project Overview

The Business Card Reader is an OCR-powered application I developed to streamline the process of digitizing physical business cards. Using computer vision and optical character recognition technology, the system automatically extracts contact information from scanned business cards, recognizes text fields such as names, phone numbers, email addresses, and company information, and organizes this data into a searchable digital contacts database. This solution eliminates manual data entry, reduces human error, and significantly improves networking efficiency for professionals and businesses.

This web-based Business Card Management System was designed to help users efficiently manage business contacts and products. It allows users to upload business card images, extract contact information, and maintain a comprehensive database of customer and product information. The system includes robust user authentication, with different permission levels for regular users and administrators.

Key Features

  • Multi-format image input (JPG, PNG, PDF) with mobile camera support
  • Automatic card detection and perspective correction
  • 95% accuracy in information extraction across diverse card designs
  • Intelligent field recognition (name, title, company, phone, email, address, website)

  • User authentication with secure login/logout functionality
  • Role-based access control (admin and regular user permissions)

Technical Implementation

The business card reader solution consists of several integrated components:

1. Image Processing & Card Detection

I developed a robust image preprocessing pipeline using OpenCV to enhance the quality of scanned or photographed business cards. The system applies adaptive thresholding, noise reduction, and contrast enhancement to optimize images for OCR. Then, I implemented a card detection algorithm that identifies the card boundaries and corrects perspective distortion, even when the card is photographed at an angle. This preprocessing significantly improves the accuracy of subsequent text recognition steps.

2. Optical Character Recognition

For text extraction, I utilized Tesseract OCR with custom training to recognize various fonts and layouts commonly used on business cards. I enhanced the engine's performance by implementing language detection to switch between recognition models based on the text's primary language. The system also handles multi-column layouts and vertical text orientations, which are common challenges in business card recognition. The result is highly accurate text extraction even from cards with complex designs and formatting.

3. Information Classification & Extraction

Once the text is extracted, I developed a sophisticated information classification system that categorizes different pieces of information. Using a combination of regular expressions, pattern matching, and heuristic rules, the application accurately identifies names, job titles, company names, phone numbers, email addresses, physical addresses, and websites. The system can distinguish between different phone number formats (mobile, office, fax) and recognize international number patterns. For ambiguous cases, the application uses contextual clues and relative positioning of information to make intelligent classifications.

4. Web Interface & Contact Management

I created a responsive web application using Flask on the backend and modern HTML/CSS/JavaScript on the frontend. The interface allows users to upload images via drag-and-drop or direct camera capture, view the extracted information with confidence scores, make any necessary corrections, and export the data in various formats. The application also includes a contact management system that allows users to organize, search, and filter their digitized contacts. The interface is designed for both desktop and mobile use, ensuring accessibility across devices.

Challenges & Solutions

Complex Card Designs

Challenge: Highly stylized business cards with unusual layouts, backgrounds, and fonts initially caused recognition errors.

Solution: Implemented advanced image segmentation techniques to isolate text regions from background elements, and trained custom OCR models on diverse card designs to improve recognition accuracy for non-standard fonts and layouts.

Field Classification Ambiguity

Challenge: Distinguishing between different types of information (e.g., determining if a number is a phone, fax, or other identifier) proved difficult in cards with minimal labeling.

Solution: Developed a context-aware classification algorithm that considers the positioning of text elements, nearby keywords, and formatting patterns to make intelligent inferences about ambiguous fields.
